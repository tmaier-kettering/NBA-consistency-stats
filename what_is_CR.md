# How Can We Measure Consistency Statistically?
## The Average Is Only Half the Story
In sports, averages are everywhere. We talk about players who score 20 points per game or grab 10 rebounds per game as a way to summarize performance.
But averages can be misleading. Two players can have the same average while performing very differently from game to game. One player might score 20 points every night with steady reliability, while another swings between 0 and 40 points. Even though their averages match, their consistency clearly does not.

## Why Variability Matters
To understand consistency, we need to look beyond the average and consider how much a player’s performance varies. This is where standard deviation comes in.
Standard deviation measures how spread out a player’s performances are around their average. A low standard deviation means the player performs similarly each game, while a high standard deviation indicates more fluctuation.
However, standard deviation alone is not enough. Players with lower averages naturally tend to have smaller standard deviations simply because they have less room to vary. For example, a player averaging 5 points per game will usually have less variation than a player averaging 20, even if both are equally “consistent” relative to their roles.

## A Better Measure: Consistency Rating
To fairly evaluate consistency, we need a metric that accounts for both average performance and variability.
One simple approach is the Consistency Rating (CR), defined as:
CR = average ÷ standard deviation (this equation needs to be formatted as an equation and put onto a UI card so that it stands out)
This ratio adjusts variability relative to a player’s typical performance. A higher CR indicates greater consistency, meaning the player delivers closer to their average more reliably. A lower CR suggests more fluctuation from game to game.
By combining average and variability into a single measure, Consistency Rating provides a clearer picture of how steady a player truly is—not just how much they produce.
